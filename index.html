Research Focus

I specialize in accretion, outflows, and relativistic jets around black holes of all mass scales, studying their relationships and impact on surroundings. My work focuses on high energy astrophysics, astroparticle physics, compact objects, magnetic fields, and jetted transients.

My research involves multi-messenger astrophysics from low-frequency radio through gamma-rays, focusing on causally connected events through VLBI and time-series analysis. I work extensively with cutting-edge instrumentation including the global mm-VLBI array (GMVA), the Event Horizon Telescope (EHT), the next-generation Event Horizon Telescope (ngEHT), POLAMI, and GeV/TeV gamma-ray facilities.

Current Research Focus

My current work focuses on understanding the physics of supermassive black holes and their jets through high-resolution imaging with the Event Horizon Telescope. I'm particularly interested in:

  • Binary black hole candidates like OJ 287
  • Magnetic field structures in AGN jets
  • Multi-wavelength variability and jet physics
  • Next-generation VLBI techniques and instrumentation
